The Mother

It is this facet that I'm (strangely) most familiar with and least aware of at the same time. Mother's are unique in their overt abstraction of the rigors of the world from their children. They go through unthinkable ills to try and guarantee the best possible destinies for them. I cannot, and won't, for one moment pretend to know the extent of suffering and selflessness they undergo to make me, us happy. Equally, I can't dare ignore the fact that they do. And we love them for that. We thank you for that. Immensely.

I thank you for the 201, 000 (and counting...) odd hours of my existence that I haven't once gone without a meal, I thank you for raising me into a life that you couldn't have dreamed of as a child, I thank you for not sleeping so that I could, I thank you for tending to me when sick and troubled, I thank you for toiling through the countless ennui-laced hours of parent's meetings and other forgettable fare, I thank you for managing me when I didn't know better, I thank you for raising me and my siblings into human beings of good and able standing, I thank you for driving long hours across dangerous roads just to see me, I thank you for loving me.

Thanks mum.
